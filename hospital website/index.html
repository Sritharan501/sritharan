Medical Care

Hospitals are often evaluated by organizations like The Joint Commission (TJC), the National Committee for Quality Assurance (NCQA), or other relevant bodies. Accreditation indicates that a hospital meets certain quality and safety standards.

Heart Care

Availability of resources and education to help patients understand their heart condition, treatment options, and lifestyle changes needed for better heart health.

Emergency Care

Emergency physicians, trauma surgeons, and emergency medical technicians (EMTs).Immediate treatment for acute conditions, injuries, or life-threatening situations. Typically provided in emergency departments (EDs) or urgent care centers.

Physical Examination

Measurement of key indicators such as blood pressure, heart rate, respiratory rate, and temperature.

Blood Tests

Common tests include a complete blood count (CBC), cholesterol levels, blood glucose levels, and liver/kidney function tests.
